via Ebay Deals

Thanksgiving in the U.S. - a time for turkey, being with loved ones, and… maps. Ebay did a survey of 15,000 people to ask about their Thanksgiving traditions and mapped out how the answers varied across the country.Â 

Of course I love the idea of mapping regional differences on any topic, and I love when unconventional sources are putting maps out into the world. But I couldn’t help but cringe when I saw this map, thinking, “Why is that the color scale?” (it has some other cartographic issues too, but let’s just stick to colors for today) For a sequential dataset, why use a rainbow color scheme? And does the color blue even show up anywhere on this map? This data would be so much easier to interpret with a color palette that went from light to dark, where dark equals more booze. Clearly it is essential that I know where in the country people drink more on Thanksgiving.

In Ebay’s defense, they did publish their data if you feel like playing around with it yourself. And they asked a whole bunch of other questions, so check out their post to see more mediocre maps.
